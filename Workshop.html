Our Workshop

Don’t know anything about bicycle repairs? No problem! Volunteer mechanics are on duty to assist you, from finding a bike that’s right for you, adjusting your seat, or fixing a flat, right up to building an entire bike from just parts. Bike Edmonton operates a fully-equipped, volunteer-run, community bike workshop. We have the tools (including specialty tools) needed to repair and maintain your bicycle, and mechanics to teach you how to do it. We have a large selection of used and new parts. We also have many used bicycles for sale, starting at $40 for basic as-is bicycles, and between $150-350 for most refurbished bikes

General Info

Bike Edmonton’s community bicycle workshop offers a welcoming space for cyclists of all skill levels to learn bike repair with guidance from volunteer mechanics. Visitors can donate used bikes or parts, purchase refurbished bikes, and find both used and select new parts.While there are some restrictions—DIY hydraulic bleeding is not permitted, nor are gas-powered bikes—Bike Edmonton supports a wide range of repairs, though its capacity for high-end components and suspension work may be limited. The workshop, one of North America's longest-running, encourages in-person visits for inventory inquiries, as they’re unable to hold items or store bikes.
